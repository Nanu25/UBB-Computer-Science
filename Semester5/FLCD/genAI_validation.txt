Question: Why I  don t need to declare the geometric operations like pythagoras and area_triangle?


Answer:
Why I don’t need to declare the geometric operations
pythagoras and area_triangle are built-in functions of the language, just like sqrt or +.
They are predefined in the interpreter, so the program already knows how to compute them.
The grammar only specifies how they are written (their syntax), not how they are implemented.



Question:
Can you give me a small example of a good program and of a bad program(a syntax error or something) for my BNF?

Answer:

✅ Good program (correct syntax)
float a;
read(a);

float b;
read(b);

write(pythagoras(a, b));
write(area_triangle(a, b));


❌ Bad program (syntax error)

float a
read(a);

write(pythagoras(a b));
❌ Errors:

Missing ; after float a → violates <declaration_statement> ::= <type> <identifier> = <expression> ;

Missing comma between a and b inside pythagoras(a b) → violates <geometry_statement> syntax.
